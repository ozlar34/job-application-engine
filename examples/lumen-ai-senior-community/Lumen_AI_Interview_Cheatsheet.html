Part 1 — Questions they'll ask

1. "How would you grow our developer community without killing the signal?"

~90sStrategy answer. Grounds in the 180K community I own now.

"The trap is chasing member count and ending up with a busy channel nobody senior wants to be in. I own a 180K+ community across Reddit, Discord, and X today, and the thing I actually optimise for is signal-to-noise, not raw size."

"So I'd start by defining what a high-signal Lumen community looks like: the people building in public, the ones answering each other's questions before we do. Then programming and moderation standards that protect that, and a regular pulse read so growth never outruns quality."

"Growth follows when the room is worth being in. That's the order I'd keep it in."

If they push on metrics → name the community intelligence pipeline: priority-tiered reports plus a daily sentiment digest, so 'health' is measured, not vibed.

2. "How would you design our champions program?"

~75sForward-looking. Proof = the Ambassador Program I built (P-LOY).

"I've built this exact thing once already. At Northwind I built the Creator Ambassador Program from scratch: tier-based on monthly Twitch and YouTube metrics, real benefits per tier. Launched with 30 creators, runs at 55 today, drives a 40% partnered-creator viewership lift in event windows."

"For Lumen the inputs change, GitHub activity and content that shows the platform doing real work instead of stream hours, but the machine is the same: identify who's already advocating, give them structure and real benefits, and make the pipeline measurable instead of ad-hoc goodwill."

If they ask about entry/exit → it's metrics plus human judgement, never pure numbers; trust and attitude fit decide the edge cases.

3. "Tell me about a high-stakes program you ran end to end."

~90sSTAR-compressed. S-001.

"The one I'd point to is a 5-day, on-site creator program for 80+ partnered creators from 12+ countries, running against a fixed broadcast schedule with no second producer to fall back on."

"I pre-built a per-creator briefing pack mapping each of 30 contracted creators to their deliverables, then ran the daily programming and vendor coordination live across the week. The contracted-creator framework produced 250+ posts, 600+ stories, and 30+ promo streams across the window."

"What I'd change: build the briefing packs a week earlier. A single shared doc per creator killed almost all the day-of questions, and more lead time would've made it smoother still."

If they probe "what was on you vs the team" → I was the only on-the-ground producer; the briefing-pack system was mine end to end.

4. "How do you actually use AI in your own work? Give me a concrete example."

~90sSTAR-compressed. S-002. This is the AI-native fluency probe — the one that matters most here.

"I don't just use AI, I ship it for a team. My 6-person EU team was losing hours a week to manual reporting, content drafting, and sentiment synthesis with no shared tooling standard."

"I defined the toolchain and a shared knowledge base of prompts and compliance SOPs, then shipped 15+ role-specific assistants, each gated by a human-in-the-loop checkpoint before anything reaches an external audience. Reclaimed roughly 8 hours a week per person, and the community intelligence pipeline ran 6+ months in production with zero manual intervention."

"The lesson: guardrails-first beat speed-first. Leading with the human-in-the-loop checkpoint is what got other teams to trust and reuse the assistants."

If they say "show me" → pivot to the GitHub tab: the reusable CLI skills and the source-agnostic automation stack.

5. "You come from gaming and consumer communities. Why developer tooling?"

~60sMotivation + the honest gap, named first so it doesn't fester.

"Fair question, and I'll name the gap myself: my community has been consumer and gaming, not developers. I'm not going to pretend otherwise."

"What bridges it is that I build with the exact class of tools Lumen ships, daily and unprompted, an agentic coding assistant as my own build platform with a library of CLI skills. So I come to a developer audience as a user of their tools, not an outsider studying them. And the community systems I've built port across audiences without a rebuild; what changes is the signal I'm reading, not the machine."

Don't over-apologise for the gap. Name it once, bridge it, move on.

6. "What are your salary expectations?" (pre-offer)

~30sDeflect → band signal → pivot. Never name a hard floor pre-offer.

"I'd rather understand the full scope and the band you've set for the level before I anchor a number, since I want to make sure we're solving the same problem."

"If it helps calibrate, I'm looking at the senior community range for Amsterdam and comparable EU-remote roles, and I weigh the whole package, equity and growth included, not just base."

Never state a specific floor figure before there's an offer on the table.

  4. "What does the first 90 days look like if this hire goes well, and what would tell you it isn't working?"
  5. Closer: "Are there any question marks about my fit I can clear up before we wrap?"
